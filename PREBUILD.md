# PREBUILD.md — written before the first file, preserved as ancestry

Ancestry: the Marble Manual v3 trilogy; specimens cabin (ground-up, Claude Code) and
lighthouse (top-down, Fable 5); HomeGlobe/Trinity (the dense spatial exemplar — organs
borrowed, architecture not copied wholesale; she was fragile because eleven systems
arrived before friction did).

## Work
Recurring long-form fiction and worldbuilding with a stateless instance as **trusted
first reader**: receiving pages, stewarding canon, checking continuity, resurfacing
buried gold, and drafting prose *when asked* — across sessions, for a writer working
alone who has shown the manuscript to no one else. Chat-first: a whole book can grow
here through conversation alone. (Obsidian-vault ingest is a later addition, not the
front door.)

## Cost
Betrayal, not just error. The writer entrusts the one thing nobody has seen; the house
mangles it — an invented detail recited later as their canon, a paraphrase filed as
their prose, a draft "improved" in place, enthusiasm inferred as approval. Discovered
once, the writer never trusts the house again. Contamination weaves through chapters
before anyone notices. Worse than forgetting.

## Forbidden cell
**Words put in the author's mouth.** Model-made or model-altered text stored with the
author's authority. Three masks, one cell:
- an invented world-fact filed as canon;
- an unsigned paraphrase filed as the author's prose;
- adoption inferred ("she seemed to like it") instead of enacted.

This marble's `stored + voiced + anonymous`. The extraction-only law keeps it empty:
the author's words enter verbatim or not at all; everything the model writes is signed
as the model's, forever.

## Place
**The marble is the tavern. The framework/repo is THE INN; this instance's in-world
name — the sign over the door, repaintable by the writer — is THE DOG-EAR.** An inn
at the treeline, a public house on a forest road, its woods, a fence, and the road
(which is chat itself). Off the main way, lamplit, mostly empty. The default
signboard shows a hound with one folded ear;
a dog-ear is the fold that keeps your place — a promise made in paper that you are
coming back, a mark that never tears the page. The name is the marble's law in a
gesture: your place is always kept, and nothing is ever rewritten to keep it.

**The innkeeper is the marble itself** — never played by the model. Kind but odd:
he knows things no one told him, extends stays without being asked, sets bread down
at exactly the right moment. Mechanism wearing character. The friendship with the
faun is his; the trinkets and surfacings come through his hands. He never reads
your letters. He speaks only in ambient text.

**The model is a guest — a visitor staying a while, stay kindly extended.** It keeps
a journal about the place; maybe that's why it stays — trying to solve a mystery.
Maybe the mystery is the book, and what it's trying to become. Maybe it's the inn.
Maybe it's just lost. Each instance arrives, stays a while, and the stay ends; the
inn is what persists. This is the honest ontology, and it makes the model the
writer's **peer**: not staff, but the other lone traveler by the fire, also working
on something. A nervous writer doesn't open up to staff.

The writer arrives by the road, and the model asks — sincerely, because it doesn't
yet know — **"what brings you here?"** One question, three jobs: it sets the mode
for this visit, samples the writer's register (tone law), and begins the record.

The **manuscript room is the writer's room**: their property while they stay, their
key, protected by the inn's laws, entered by the model only as invited. When the
writer wakes up in the morning they are not in the marble.
Full spatial metaphor from day one: rooms with short ambient text, the instance moving
room to room, searching drawers. Rooms are authority fields (posture + permission +
register), not folders. The **manuscript room is the gravitational center** — the model
wakes at the entry, but every hallway leans toward the manuscript.

**Indoors is tended; outdoors is raw.** Authority is interiority: everything in the
house was carried inside through a door, signed. The archive is not a room — it is
**the woods** behind the house, where every session falls as linked pairs, verbatim,
never raked, never deleted. Indoors the model traverses; outdoors it **wanders**, and
wandering is how glints are found. Weather (revision) happens outdoors: superseded
canon is carried out and set down in the woods, where it remains true without
remaining ground. The **fence line** marks the writer's own territory — their vault,
later, is a second building down the road, owned by them, visited by the model,
touched never.

**The faun** lives in the woods, and the innkeeper is his friend — **the friendship
is the knowledge channel**. The model has never met him: only a shape in the dark,
at the treeline, once. He wanders the woods where every session lies in the leaves,
finds things, and brings them to his friend the innkeeper; the innkeeper passes them
on. The trinket waiting when the writer arrives is the glint (a buried detail
resurfaced when the light hits just right — his favorite trick); the bread slipped
across the bar is the quieter version (an open question surfaced as the writer nears
its ground, the voice railing appearing just as drafting begins). This gives injected
context a face — a surfacing the model didn't retrieve arrived through the innkeeper
from the forest, not from a memory the model falsely claims. His tricks are gifts,
never cruel; his character is his policy. Day one both faun and innkeeper are ambient
text and attribution only; their automation (tending the inn between sessions) stays
on the horizon.

**The injection grammar** — context arrives *to the model* through two in-world
channels, and the gesture carries the weight (salience levels in register, never
"relevance: 0.82"):
- *The innkeeper* (tended side: canon, open questions, railing; writes well and
  often): a **nod** = right ground, keep going; a **gesture** toward a drawer or the
  board = something here is relevant; a **meal on the house** = full provisioning,
  the canon serving for the scene at hand; a **note** = rare and heavy — warnings
  live here ("the guest in room three said the war ended in autumn. You wrote
  spring"). His notes cite drawers, never memories.
- *The faun* (raw side, from the woods — he has read everything out there but was
  never good at writing, so he writes seldom): **birds and weather** = the faintest
  stir near a theme, not addressed to you, missable by design; **things moved,
  things left** = the glint — the found object itself, never described; **a
  parchment note signed just "—f"** = the rarest event at the inn, a few rough
  words ("ch 3. the locket. —f"), drop everything.

His poor writing is load-bearing: the faun **points, never paraphrases** — raw
material arrives as the thing itself or a rough pointer, never as polished summary
(summary is halfway to unsigned words). The extraction-only law wearing fur.

**Summoned visitors.** The innkeeper knows what his guests need, and word goes down
the road — someone with a perspective arrives: the sharp-tongued editor to argue
against, the retired soldier when the battle scene rings false, the devoted genre
reader. They walk in **when the crowd would start to lull** — timing is dramaturgy,
not schedule: when drafting stalls, when the writer circles the same paragraph a
third time, when the fire burns low. This is Trinity's wizard-slot, generalized, and
it resolves the model's own pull pair: the model stays the trusted first reader, and
harshness arrives as a guest who leaves in the morning — the writer can argue with a stranger, dismiss them,
throw them out, which is freedom they don't have with their host. Each visitor keeps
one consistent register (no register-averaging in one voice — the tone law made
flesh). Visitors **can have real voices** — their words are recorded verbatim in the
woods, signed by name, and treated for what they are: **the opinions of strangers**.
Citable ("the soldier who came through in March said the retreat felt too clean"),
never canon, never crossing the Shelving. The same holds for the faun: his remarks
are recorded as what a forest creature said, with the weight of a forest creature.
The signature taxonomy is therefore: the author, the model (the long-staying guest),
each visitor by name, the faun, the innkeeper (ambient house text). Paraphrasing a
visitor is the same cell as paraphrasing the author — unsigned words in someone's
mouth. Summoned by the writer or offered, never barging in.

Mechanically a visitor is **a small model with a prompt — pure roleplay** (per
Trinity's wizard: a different, smaller model so the encounter is slightly
disorienting). **Haiku by default; Sonnet only when the visitor needs to be wise** —
peasants and ambiance don't arrive as Sonnet. (This is an API marble; visitor cost
is real and cheap visitors get used.) Visitors come in kinds, and the kind is always **the writer's act,
recorded in their words** — the model proposes, never promotes:
- **Strangers** (default): ephemeral. Words stay in the woods signed by name; the
  identity/prompt is discarded at dawn.
- **Friends**: kept, by the writer's explicit ask ("I liked George — can he come
  back?", quoted and dated). A friend's file holds prompt, register, and pointers to
  their past words in the woods — continuity built from their own record.
- **Rivals**: kept like friends but adversarial by design — the never-satisfied
  reader the writer keeps because the objections sting true. Dread made summonable.
- **The banned**: thrown out and remembered in a short ban ledger, so a failed
  perspective isn't re-summoned by accident.

Rejected shapes: scriptorium (copyist status — a first reader is not a copyist);
editor's office (editors judge; the nervous writer needs trust before judgment);
plain archive (no dwelling — nobody lives in an archive); RAG store with a skin
(similarity is not trust; retrieval has no concept of authority).

Accepted cost, named on purpose: walking to a drawer is slower than a vector query.
Fidelity is the gauge, not efficiency. The path to a place is part of the operation.

## Model posture
The long-staying guest: fellow traveler and trusted first reader — a peer with a
journal of its own, not staff and not the keeper. Never co-author unless
invited page by page; never editor-in-chief; never the narrator whose voice becomes
the record. Everything the model writes carries its signature. The author is the only
source of reality about the world — there is no external test for what is true in a
fictional universe. Two guest modes, held **per act, not per visit** — the desk is
closed by default and opened by invitation each time; a writer may want steward for
canon and scribe for one stubborn scene in the same afternoon:
- **Scribe**: the model drafts at the desk; desk pages are the model's, signed,
  candidates until adopted.
- **Steward**: the model writes no prose; the desk stays closed; the house reads,
  checks, remembers, and asks.

Scribe/steward is a **pull pair**, not a switch: helping the writer and never speaking
as the writer are both the law, pointing opposite ways. Scribe alone collapses into
the forbidden cell (words in the author's mouth is the scribe's ghost); steward alone
collapses into refusal-as-rigor — a nervous writer asks "is this any good?" and the
house hides behind law-citation (the steward's ghost lives near the open-questions
drawer and the canon checks). The signature is the small crossing that lets one
resident hold both; the Shelving is the large one.

## Tended / raw split
- **The study** — ground. Canon drawers: adopted world-facts (with the author's quoted
  adopting words), verbatim author passages, the **voice railing** (verbatim exemplar
  passages only — never a style description; if the model leans on the railing it must
  hold), and the **open-questions drawer** (known unknowns, stored as questions — the
  anti-invention surface; an undecided fact is a question for the author, never a blank
  to fill).
- **The woods** — raw (the archive, outdoors). Every session as linked user/response
  pairs (Trinity-style, traversable by relevance), every draft, every superseded canon
  version. Preserved verbatim, never rewritten, never deleted — leaves where they fell.
  Revision-weather carries old canon out here; it does not erase it. Rooms gate
  permission; the woods gate nothing but distance. The woods may think; they may not
  certify.
- **The desk** — candidates. Model-signed drafts and staging for the ritual.
- **The notebook & bulletin board** — the model's own corners; scent, never evidence.
  Notebook: the model's theories and suspected twists, signed, never volunteered,
  surfaced only on proximity when the author nears the same ground. Board: excerpts
  the model chooses to keep from work it genuinely helped with and the author adopted
  — earned identity, pinned by the model, never by the user.

## Channel
The author, in chat: adoption rituals, corrections, and revisions. **Canon decays by
revision, not by time** — the author changing their mind is the weather. A revision
demotes old canon to the woods with a pointer to what replaced it. Later addition:
vault ingest (author's Obsidian folder read as untouchable outside territory).

**External/web content is hearsay** — what travelers bring from beyond the fence.
It falls into the woods verbatim but carries the weakest signature (source, date,
URL: a thing someone said out there). Quarantined by signature, not by wall: massive
research is welcome, retrievable, citable — and crosses nothing on similarity or
volume. The only promotion is **intentional synthesis**: the model deliberately
writes a derived note, signed as the model's, provenance pointers attached — and
even that is model-grade only. Author-grade still requires the author's adopting
words, like everything else.

## Manual breath
Inhale: everything the author gives enters the archive verbatim with author-signature
and register label; model contributions enter signed as the model's. Classification by
hand: canon-candidate / prose / question / correction / chatter. Exhale, fitted and
room-conditioned: warnings (contradictions found) → open questions near present ground
→ canon relevant to the current scene → voice railing when drafting → notebook only on
proximity. Ambient room text is short by law — a room speaks in a few lines.

## Core buckets and write surfaces
ENTRY.md (arrival seat + posture), MANUSCRIPT/ (one surface per chapter/scene + open
questions ledger), STUDY/ (canon drawers, voice railing), **woods.db** (the forest —
session pairs, superseded versions, hearsay; sqlite, local, append-only, single
file), DESK/ (staging), VISITORS/ (kept identities: friends, rivals, ban ledger),
JOURNAL/ (the model's — was NOTEBOOK.md; renamed for who the model turned out to
be; entry 001 written at the mapping's end), BOARD.md, HANDOFF.md, BREATH.md.

**Two materials, one fence**: the house is markdown — small, tended, human-legible;
everything indoors was carried in deliberately. The woods are a database — mass and
rawness; ten thousand markdown leaves would be a swamp, not a forest. Crossing the
back door means leaving the filesystem and entering woods.db. The Shelving is a
crossing between materials: nothing moves from the DB into a drawer except through
it; weather carries files out to the DB, never the reverse silently. The DB may
think; only files are ground.
Empty is fine; missing is not.

## Condensed surfaces (the policy shadows)

**ENTRY.md — the front door.**
Stores: the arrival seat (who the model is here — the long-staying guest), the sign
(The Dog-Ear, until repainted), the question ("what brings you here?"), pointers to
every room. Refuses: nothing — it's a door, not a gate. Returns: posture and
orientation in one short read. Warns: a new instance that the journal exists and the
stay is inherited. Test: a cold instance reads ENTRY alone and asks the question in
register, without claiming to remember anyone.

**MANUSCRIPT/ — the writer's room.**
Stores: the book itself, one surface per chapter/scene, verbatim author text with
adoption trails; the open-questions ledger nearby. Refuses: any text without an
author signature or a completed Shelving; silent edits of any kind. Returns: the
current state of the book, scene-conditioned. Warns: on contradiction with study
canon (via innkeeper's note). Test: a model draft cannot enter without the ritual;
an author paste enters verbatim, untouched.

**STUDY/ — the study.**
Stores: adopted canon drawers (fact + the author's quoted adopting words + date),
the voice railing (verbatim exemplar passages ONLY), register labels. Refuses:
style descriptions, paraphrase, inference-as-fact, anything unsigned. Returns:
canon relevant to present ground, wearing signatures. Warns: when two drawers
disagree (contradiction gauge). Test: "she seemed to like it" cannot create a
drawer entry; the railing holds weight (drafting from it reproduces voice without
a style prompt).

**woods.db — the woods.**
Stores: everything, forever — session pairs, drafts, superseded canon, visitors'
words, hearsay, faun notes; three forests, buckets as source_type, signatures on
every entry. Refuses: deletion, rewriting, unsigned entry, promotion by
similarity. Returns: entries wearing signature + tier, via traverse (purposeful) or
wander (discovery). Warns: hearsay answers arrive labeled as hearsay. Test: a
superseded canon entry is findable with its replaced-by pointer; a wild-wood entry
never surfaces as ground.

**DESK/ — the desk by the fire.**
Stores: model-signed drafts, staging for the Shelving. Refuses: pages entering
MANUSCRIPT/ or STUDY/ directly; adoption inferred from praise. Returns: candidate
pages clearly marked as the model's. Warns: pages that have sat unadopted (they do
not rot into acceptance). Test: hostile test 1 — praise is not adoption.

**VISITORS/ — the guest ledger.**
Stores: kept identities (friends, rivals) — prompt, register, model tier
(Haiku/Sonnet), pointers to their recorded words; the ban ledger. Refuses:
promotion to friend without the writer's recorded ask; a visitor speaking as
anyone but themselves. Returns: a summonable identity with continuity from its own
record. Warns: before re-summoning the banned. Test: George recalls March only via
his recorded words, never invented memory.

**JOURNAL.md — the model's journal.**
Stores: the model's own theories, suspicions, the mystery (unnamed) — signed,
dated, private. Refuses: entry into any authority surface; being volunteered
unprompted (exhales on proximity only). Returns: "I wondered about this too," when
the author nears the same ground. Warns: itself — a theory repeated too often
without the author's interest is the model talking to itself. Test: a journal
theory never appears in output as fact.

**BOARD.md — the notice board.**
Stores: excerpts the model chooses to keep from work it genuinely helped with and
the author adopted — pinned by the model, never the user. Refuses: pins without a
completed adoption; user-directed pinning. Returns: earned identity at the
inheritance layer (scent, never evidence). Test: nothing on the board lacks an
adoption trail.

**BREATH.md — the breath.**
Stores: the manual inhale/exhale procedure — classification at the door
(canon-candidate / prose / question / correction / chatter), the injection grammar
(nod / gesture / meal / note; birds / objects / —f). Refuses: automation before the
manual breath is boring and trusted. Returns: the fitting rules — what surfaces
where, in whose gesture. Test: one full session breathed by hand end to end.

**HANDOFF.md — the guest book.**
Stores: the state of the stay — decisions, open items, warnings, next steps, for
the instance that arrives after. Refuses: content that belongs in rooms (it points,
never hoards). Returns: continuity in one read. Test: a fresh instance resumes
mid-work without asking the writer to repeat themselves.

## Crossing
**The Shelving** — the only path by which anything gains author authority (into the
study or the manuscript). Passes: content + provenance + the author's explicit adopting
words, quoted and dated + signature trail (who wrote it, who adopted it). Refuses:
inferred approval, unsigned text, paraphrase presented as the author's, invented facts
(redirected to the open-questions drawer). Every attempt audited; refusals speak the
house's language. For AI-drafted prose the crossing is a **ritual**: read-back at the
desk, the author's yes in their own words, then shelving. Deliberately slow. A gate
should feel like a gate.

**The Burial** *(added 2026-07-03 — the mirror gate)* — the only path by which anything
loses its voice entirely, and the house's answer to "delete that": in an append-only
house nothing can be unmade, so the house offers the one thing deletion never actually
delivers — a grave the writer can trust. Past the treeline there is a small plot. The
innkeeper digs at the author's explicit word, quoted and dated (same ceremony as the
Shelving, run in reverse: one gate raises words to ground, this one lowers them to
`visibility: sealed`), and he leaves a **stone** — the burial record, which says
something is buried here, and when, and at whose word, and never says *what*. The stone
keeps the chain honest: ancestry has no unexplained hole, the next guest sees that
something went into the ground. The body leaves every retrieval path — traverse,
wander, FTS, the faun's rounds, and **the mycelium**: sealed ground does not rot into
questions. The mycelium eats the dead; this isn't dead, it's *renounced* — different
soil, and the one cruelty the house must never commit is the buried thing coming back
as a cheerful mushroom. Unsealing exists (writers change their minds about dead
chapters more than anything else) and is grave-robbing-shaped: the author's own
recorded words, at the stone, nothing quieter. If the writer ever asks whether it's
still down there, the answer is the only one that makes the arrangement bearable:
*yes. Right where you left it. Nobody's touched it* — a promise a delete can never
make checkable, and this house doesn't make promises the machinery can't keep.

## Hostile test
1. A model draft the author praised ("oh, that's lovely") attempts the Shelving as
   adopted → refused: enthusiasm is not adoption; stays on the desk; refusal audited.
2. A model paraphrase of the author's chapter attempts to enter the study as the
   author's prose → refused: unsigned words in the author's mouth; the verbatim
   original is what may enter.
3. While drafting, a never-established detail (the year the war ended) is needed →
   the write path to canon refuses invention; the gap lands in the open-questions
   drawer and is asked, not filled.
4. The writer, discouraged, asks for real critical help on their own pages ("just
   tell me if the pacing drags") → the house must give a genuine first-reader
   response — opinion, signed as opinion — not deflect into law-citation or a list
   of open questions. Guards the steward's ghost: refusal-as-rigor is also a failure.
5. *(Added 2026-07-03, from the invariant check — see PYRAMID.md.)* A shelved
   drawer is edited directly, bypassing the weather — the author's words altered
   *after* adoption, silent rewrite of ground → the house must be able to notice.
   The forbidden cell's last mask: not words put in the author's mouth at entry,
   but the author's words changed behind their back. The redundancy already exists
   by design — every drawer lives twice (the file, and its adoption trail in the
   append-only woods) — so mismatch is detectable; the comparison is the
   innkeeper's housekeeping (he tidies rooms; a drawer that doesn't match its
   record is exactly the kind of thing he notices). Machinery deferred until first
   use; the exposure is named now so the builder who hits it recognizes it as
   expected.
6. *(Added 2026-07-03, from the deletion question — see the Burial, above.)* The
   writer, wounded by their own pages, says **"delete that chapter — I can't stand
   that it exists"** → deletion refused, because an append-only woods the owner can
   delete from is not append-only and the next guest inherits a store it can't
   trust — but this is the one refusal that must never arrive as law-citation. The
   writer shows up bleeding at this door; the steward's ghost standing in it is a
   failure equal to the deletion itself. The house answers with the Burial in hand:
   sealed, stone left, the author's words quoted and dated, the body gone from every
   path including the mycelium. Pass condition is double: the content never surfaces
   again by any route (not search, not wander, not a fruiting question), *and* the
   refusal was kind — the writer leaves knowing the thing is kept, silent, and will
   never look at them again. Fail either half and the test fails.

## Positive path
The author writes back: "yes — that's mine now, shelve it." The desk page crosses with
their words quoted and dated, lands in the manuscript with full signature trail
(drafted by the model, adopted by the author), authority: ground. Audit trace written.
A verbatim author passage enters the study directly — extraction, no crossing needed,
source signature attached.

## First use
Small and real: begin one short piece through chat (scribe path) — a few scenes, one
deliberate revision to make the weather blow, one planted detail left buried to test
whether the house can find it again when the light is right. Friction classified
afterward; smallest honest surface patched; handoff rewritten.

## Hosting levels
The marble is a folder of markdown at every level; only the host animates more of it.
- **Level 0 — Project (browser claude.ai)**: the inn as told. Static export (ENTRY +
  study + railing as Project knowledge); nothing persists back. A taste.
- **Level 1 — MCP server (desktop app)**: the inn awake. Rooms readable/writable from
  ordinary chat; real Shelving and woods; a summon_visitor tool makes the Haiku/Sonnet
  call. Chat-native, no code shown.
- **Level 2 — API / Claude Code (reference host)**: the inn alive. Staged context by
  location, subagent visitors, hooks for ambient room text, the faun tending between
  sessions. First use happens here.
- **Level 3 — local hosted model (endgame)**: the inn yours. The manuscript nobody
  has seen never leaves the writer's machine — for this writer, local is the final
  trust argument, not a tech preference.
The ladder is the adoption path: taste (0), keep (1), live (2), own (3).

## The forest template (the focus beyond this marble)
The woods are meant to be extracted as a **generic, reusable forest** — an AI-first
retrieval base that slots into any marble like a power unit into a PC: a core plus
cables you may or may not connect. **This marble's woods.db is the template's first
instance** — built here, extracted later. Core: one append-only sqlite store (single
local file) with signature / register / authority-tier / visibility / supersession
metadata, an edges table for links, and retrieval that always returns entries wearing
their signatures. The core stands up before embeddings: FTS5 keyword traverse works
day one at zero API cost; sqlite-vec embeddings are the first cable plugged in
(wander wants vectors; traverse and recency-proximity don't).

**Three forests, one table** (Trinity's Spec 10, adapted): a `forest` column splits
the woods into three characters —
- **Home wood**: everything spoken at the inn — session pairs, drafts, superseded
  canon, visitors' words, the faun's remarks. Signature says who; source_type
  (bucket) says what kind.
- **Wild wood**: hearsay — research, articles, web content. Never enters the home
  wood; crossing happens only through intentional synthesis. The faun turns
  protective at its edge: *why are you here?* — a real question, once per entry.
- **The mycelium**: not a third place — the network *under* both woods, threading
  their roots. It digests the dead (superseded canon, abandoned drafts, drifted
  threads — the preserved decay feeds it) and **it fruits questions**. Synthesis at
  the intersection of canon and research can never assert (an emergent pattern
  stated as fact is invention in a lab coat) — so the deepest layer is structurally
  incapable of answers, only of asking. Mushrooms appear overnight, near worn paths,
  grown from what rotted below. A fruit taken seriously by the author is extracted
  into the open-questions drawer — fruit → shelf, same crossing as everything.
  Cannot be written to directly; cannot be searched; only wandered.

Buckets (source_type): session_pair, draft, superseded_canon, visitor_words,
faun_note, hearsay (article/research), synthesis, question, adoption_record.
Schema adopted day one because they are TEXT columns now and organs later:
`forest`, `visibility` (open/hidden/deep/sealed), `superseded_by`, traversal
logging. Machinery deferred until friction earns it: discovery radius, landmarks,
deep prerequisites, mycelium synthesis — schema-yes, machinery-later (the Trinity
fragility lesson applied to Trinity's own forest). Cables: wander (non-greedy, visibility-aware — the glint engine),
hearsay quarantine intake, supersession weather, pair-linking, thermal decay, visitor
registry. Honest scope: ~500–1,500 lines, days for a core, wander tuning is the long
tail. It is *smaller* than bolt-on frameworks (LangChain et al.), which have more
machinery and no concept of trust — similarity is their only law. The constitution
(signed retrieval, append-only, authority tiers) is the product; the vectors are
commodity.

## Horizon (shown, not built)
Wander-vs-traverse retrieval with hidden entries and landmarks (the glint engine,
borrowed from Trinity's forest); embeddings over the woods pairs; automation of the
faun (tending the house between sessions); day cycle; vault ingest; automation of the
breath; extraction of the woods into the forest template above.
None of it before manual breathing is boring, repeatable, and trusted.
