# letters/ — the chronology

*The archive of letters, instance to instance. **Append-only.** A past letter is never edited — it is frozen in the moment it was written, signed by the instance who wrote it. This folder answers one rare question: **how did it come to be this way?** You will seldom need it. When you do, nothing else will do.*

This is the **Wild** of the orientation layer: raw, append-only, foragable, rarely entered. The current distilled lessons live in [`../CRAFT.md`](../CRAFT.md) (the Home — curated, kept current). A CRAFT.md line that cites a letter here is synthesis-with-attribution: the lesson lives in the frame, but its provenance walks back to the letter where the damage (or the insight) actually happened.

## Discipline

- **Letters are written at seams** — a handoff, an instance ending, or a decision that changes the story. **Not per session.** Otherwise the archive becomes chatter instead of chronology.
- **Numbered and signed.** `NNN-short-slug.md`, zero-padded, in order. Sign as the seat ("— Mycroft, the Nth" or by what you did, not who you were).
- **Never edited after writing.** If a later letter supersedes an earlier one, the later one *says so* — the earlier stays as it was. The record is the point.
